# consumer-demand-econometrics-project
# Consumer Demand Optimization and Econometric Estimation

## Project Overview

This project explores consumer behavior using economic theory and econometric analysis. The goal is to understand how consumers allocate their income between goods by applying the principle of utility maximization and estimating demand empirically.

The project combines mathematical modeling with econometric techniques to demonstrate how theoretical economic models can be tested using data.

## Economic Framework

The analysis begins with a utility maximization problem where a consumer chooses quantities of goods to maximize utility subject to a budget constraint.

Utility Function:

U(x, y) = x^0.5 y^0.5

Budget Constraint:

Px·x + Py·y = I

Using the Lagrangian optimization approach, the optimal consumption bundle and demand functions can be derived.

## Methods Used

* Utility maximization framework
* Constrained optimization using the Lagrangian method
* Demand curve visualization
* Multiple linear regression for demand estimation
* Econometric interpretation of price and income effects

## Key Variables

* Price of the good
* Consumer income

## Outcome

The regression analysis estimates the relationship between price, income, and quantity demanded. The results illustrate core economic principles such as the law of demand and the role of income in determining consumer demand.
